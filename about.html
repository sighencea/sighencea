DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

[COGNITIVE LOAD]

Systems should reduce cognitive load. If the user has to think harder because of your system, you've failed.

[INTENTIONAL COMPLEXITY]

Complexity must be intentional. Every layer of abstraction needs to justify its existence.

[RELIABILITY]

Reliability is a feature. It's not something you add later — it's built in from the start.

[REAL USAGE]

Software should endure real usage, not ideal assumptions. Design for the messy reality.

I work with founders, teams, and organisations who care about getting it right the first time.
