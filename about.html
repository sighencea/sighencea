THE ORIGIN

[BOOT_SEQUENCE_01]
It started in Romania, where resilience wasn't a choice—it was a requirement. The real shift happened when I left home for Ireland with one suitcase and no clear plan, only the conviction that I wanted to build something real with my hands.

[BOOT_SEQUENCE_02]
I landed in Kinsale, Ireland. Under the mentorship of Danny, I first touched leather with serious intent: cutting, skiving, and stitching my first pair of shoes. I learned that a single pair of shoes can carry the story of a man.

THE ENGINEER

Escape the Pacific

GAME

Worked on game logic and in-game economy balancing. As a Product Designer, I learned the hard way that a system is only as strong as its weakest constraint, and that players will always find it. Balancing thousands of emergent micro-decisions taught me more about real-world behavior than any classroom ever could.

VISIT WEBSITE

Abrax

SAAS

I architected a maintenance system for teams managing dozens of properties and recurring jobs. Every task has a lifecycle, an audit trail, and a next scheduled moment — no forgotten work, no spreadsheet hell, no silent failures. If a human has to remember a task, the system has already failed.

VISIT WEBSITE

Sinest

APP

I designed a household and family management platform for overwhelmed parents who were drowning in chaos: calendars, chores, medical needs, routines, messages. SINEST turns the "mental load" into an observable system — tasks become shared responsibilities, not silent burdens. The goal is simple: families should function like teams, not emergencies. In homes, just like in code, entropy is the enemy.

VALUE SYSTEM

[CRAFT]

Doing it right when no one is watching. The back of the leather must be as clean as the front. The first pair of shoes I ever made in Kinsale tore at the heel — so I rebuilt them from scratch instead of hiding the mistake.

[INTEGRITY]

I do not sell what I would not use. My word is my bond, in code and in contract. When a client asked me to "just paint over" cracked leather to make it look new, I refused; I repaired it properly or not at all.

[LONGEVITY]

"Choose one, choose once." I build for the next generation, not the next season. A belt I hand-stitched in 2019 is still worn daily by its owner — the patina has changed, the stitching has not.

[BROTHERHOOD]

We rise by lifting others. My knowledge is open source, my workshop has an open door. I once returned a restored family wallet in person; the owner cried. That's why I share this craft instead of guarding it.
